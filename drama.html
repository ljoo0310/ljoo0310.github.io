Little Forest, 2018 (리틀 포레스트)
  • Director: Yim Soon-rye
  • Release Date: February 28th, 2018
  • Starring: Kim Tae-ri, Ryu Jun-yeol, Moon So-ri
More Info

"Only I came back without having found anything."

Studies, dating, job-hunting, nothing works out for Hye-won as she lives her busy city life. After much exhaustion, she decides to go back home in the country side where she meets her old friends, Jae-ha and Eun-suk. It was an impuslive decision, to go back home as if running away from the reality facing her in Seoul. Not sure what to do with her life, she spends time with Jae-ha, who also came back from the city but with determination to live a farming life to do what he truly enjoys, and Eun-suk, who is working at the local bank and is satisfied with a stable job. Getting her mind off of everything, she relaxes in nature and reflects much upon herself. As she spends a special year there and finally it's spring again, she makes her first step to a new chapter of her life.


This film is based on the original movie "Little Forest: Four Seasons" which was made in Japan. I personally haven't watched the Japaneese version yet, but I enjoyed this one as it reflected well of what many Korean young adults worry today: what on earth to do with our lives. Getting a job is harder than ever before, and it seems we're living meaningless lives. Little Forest gives us a nice break from our stressful and busy workalholic lives and have us think about ourselves deeply once more. Plus, the food taht Hye-won cooks look absolutely delicious, so make sure you have a full stomach before watching or plan a big meal ahead afterwards.


The King of Jokgu, 2013 (족구왕)
  • Director: Woo Moon-gi
  • Release Date: August 21st, 2014
  • Starring: Ahn Jae-hong, Hwang Seung-eon, Jung Woo-sik
More Info

"I also think that it's stupid to live while hiding what you like just because others don't like it."

Man-sup has just came back to college at 24-years old, sucks at studying, and is unpopular among girls. And when everyoen else is busy studying for entrance exams, all he thinks about is the hottest girl on campus and how he wants to play Jokgu. Almost everyone avoids him at first as they don't want to get involved with him. But as the school decides to demolish the Jokgu field, he goes on protest and does his best to bring back Jokgu into his campus. Gradually, some people follow along as they desire to get away from the stress of studying, and soon enough, everyone becomes a fan of Jokgu and Man-sup is famous as the guy who brought it back. There's no mateiral reward in succeeding to keeping the Jokgu field in place but to Man-sup, that's not the thing that matters.


First off, Jokgu is a mix of soccer and volley ball. You basically play volley ball but you only use your feet to pass and spike the ball. It's a relatively popular sport among guys in Korea, but is less appealing to women who'd mostly rather prefer soccer. So Man-sup is the guy who's going against social conventions and even I wouldn't want to be where he is. I'm a realistic kind of guy so I can't deny the importance of studying at college. But to see how Man-sup never get discouraged by what others say and is adamant in achieving what he wants was really quite inpiring. He's a fool in the movie, but perhaps it's fools who are the ones who can do more than the ordinary.


Scandal Makers, 2008 (과속스캔들)
  • Director: Kang Hyeong-cheol
  • Release Date: December 3rd, 2008
  • Starring: Cha Tae-hyun, Park Bo-young, Wang Seok-hyeon
More Info

"Why should I be gone? I'm here, right now, but why should I be gone?!?"

Nam Hyun-soo is a well-going celebrity, was popular among teens in back in the days and now in his 30's he's the nuumber #1 radio DJ with a renowned reputation. Everything in his life seems perfect until one day, he gets a story on broadcast about someone looking for her father. He encouraged her to go find him but then suddenly, a girl (Hwang Jung-nam) and a young boy come up to his apartment, saying that she's his daughter and he's his grandchild. Apparently, he had her when he was just a teen and now his daughter too gave birth to a boy when she was a teen. His manager warns him that celebrities who had scandals go down the drain without doubt, so he tries to avoid her. But Jung-nam, having sought for her dad for so long is not a quitter. However, eventually social media catches on and Hyun-soo is on the risk of losing all of his reputation. He's in real trouble.


Although not exactly the most relatable story, it's nonetheless a realistic one so it's not entirely strange to the audience. And the little boy is just too cute not to watch. Stepping back from the twisted movie plot, the film throws us the question of which matters more, your career or your family. The answer should be obvious to most of us but I'm not sure how many of us will think the same when that family suddenly comes out of nowhere after more than a decade has past. Hyun-soo struggles with this question as he surely has a lot to lose. As to what he decides upon, I'll leave that up to you to figure out.
